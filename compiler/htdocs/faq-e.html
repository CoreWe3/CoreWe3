Why did you choose SPARC assembly for the target language?

Because IA-32 is a mess (at least for me and for the purpose of education) and Sun is still strong in universities around me.

Why don't you generate byte code / C / C-- / GCC's RTL / (you name it)?

Because they hide too many important details and require extra knowledge. Generating an intermediate language shows only some part of compilers' work. In addition, there is a not-so-small overhead of learning the intermediate language itself.

In other words, consider the following story: you ask me how to get to Tokyo from New York; I tell you to fly to Moscow. You not only have to find the way from Moscow to Tokyo, but also are making a long detour. In addition, you have to learn Russian to get through there. (OK, maybe not in real Moscow, but you get the idea.)

Could you add polymorphism / data types / pattern matching / garbage collection / modules / etc.?

I'd love to, but there is a tradeoff between simplicity and functionality (even in ML). Adding those features would have made the compiler too complex for the original audience—that is, undergraduate students. On the other hand, it would be nice projects for more advanced audience (such as graduate students).

If you are interested in a compiler with more functionality and complexity than MinCaml (but less than OCaml), take a look at Caml Light and its variants.
